10.2 10.3 | Research Article title

Research article titles usually include Topic, Field, Methods and Result.

Example:

  • Topic + Field

    The social dilemma of autonomous vehicles

  • Topic + Method

    Generating Light from Darkness

  • Topic + Field + Broad Field

    Geriatric Conditions in a Population-Based Sample of Older Homeless Adults

The titles of Chinese academic papers often include “the investigation of … “, “analysis of …” and other expressions are redundant in English academic papers. What English stresses is to come straight to the point.

Sentences:

==Study on== suppression of aeroelastic instability of 2-D wing by nonlinear energy sinks.

A high efficient (==highly efficient / high efficiency==) apparatus using solar energy.

Switching angle revise (==revision==) diagram.

Effect of blockage ratio on spanwise-averaged overall cooling effectiveness under (==with==) high blowing ratio.

Spanwise-averaged adiabatic film cooling effectiveness distribution (==Distribution of cooling effectiveness of spanwise-averaged adiabatic film==) on the flat plate.

Computational complexity comparison among different algorithms → Table 1 Comparison of computational complexity of different algorithms

Research of simulation techniques for large civil aircraft’s fuel weight properties → Simulation techniques for analyzing fuel weight properties of large civil aircrafts

Schematic waveform of AC filter inductor current when load is shorted (==with shorted load==).

There has been a marked increase in the use of main and subtitles in English journal articles in many fields of study.

Compound titles:

  1. Compound titles occupy a high proportion in the titles of journal papers in the field of computer science. The main title adopts the most concise form of abbreviations, which can be eye-catching and easy to remember.

    From Content to Context**:** The Evolution and Growth of Data Quality Research

    One Size Does Not Fit All**—**A Contingency Approach to Data Governance

    Why are some verbs learned before other verbs**?** Effects of input frequency and structure on children’s early verb use

  2. In order to highlight the theme, a compound heading structure is usually used. Put the key words that represent the research focus of the paper in the main heading, and supplement and explain the topic in the subheading.

    Traditional Chinese Medicine**:** Problems and Pitfalls

    Poly(benzobisoxazole divinylene): synthesis, photophysical properties, and electron paramagnetic resonance studies (Polymer Chemistry)

    Hybrid Reynolds-Averaged/Large-Eddy Simulation of a Cavity Flameholder**:** Modeling Sensitivities (Aeronautics and astronautics)

    Quantum Hall physics**:** Hierarchies and conformal field theory techniques (Physic)

  3. The author can highlight the research topic in the main title, but also give readers a clear view of the stylistic characteristics or research methods of the paper through the subtitle.

    Implementing foreign language curriculum innovation in a Chinese secondary school**:** An ethnographic study on teacher cognition and classroom practices

    The Use of Technology in Higher Education Programs**:** a National Survey (pedagogy)

    Seismic Risk Assessment of Historic Masonry Towers**:** Comparison of Four Case Studies (Civil engineering)

    Computational Dynamics of Flapping Wings in Hover Flight**:** A Co-Simulation Strategy (Aeronautics and astronautics)

    Weak Form Quadrature Element Method and Its Applications in Science and Engineering**:** A State-of-the-Art Review (Mechanical engineering)

Be sure to consider the type of article and its field when using compound titles.

10.17 Abstract

==IMRD==: Introduction—Method—Result—Discussion

The abstract should make the content of the article quickly clear to the readers.

Context

Software practitioners are often the primary source of information for software engineering research. They naturally produce information about their experiences of software practice, and the beliefs they infer from their experiences. Researchers must evaluate the quality and quantity of this information for their research.

Objective

To examine how concepts and methods from argumentation research can be used to study practitioners’ evidence, inference and beliefs so as to better understand and improve software practice.

Method

We develop a preliminary framework and preliminary methodology, and use those to identify, extract and structure practitioners’ evidence, inference and beliefs. We illustrate the application of the framework and methodology with examples from a practitioner’s blog post.

Result

The practitioner uses (factual) stories, analogies, examples and popular opinion as evidence, and uses that evidence in defeasible reasoning to justify his beliefs and to rebut the beliefs of other practitioners.

Conclusion

The framework, methodology and examples could provide a foundation for software engineering researchers to develop a more sophisticated understanding of, and appreciation for, practitioners’ defeasible evidence, inference and belief. Further work needs to automate (parts of) the methodology to support larger-scale application of the methodology.

Structured abstracts have been widely used in the field of medicine and health since 1980s, and are also used in many journals of natural and social sciences. This summary is characterized by subheadings at the head of each section. Context, Objective, Method, Result, Conclusion, etc.

12.23 | Speech

​ Good afternoon, everybody. Maybe you don’t know the material, but you must have heard of graphene. You’ve never even heard of graphene, but you must know the source of life – carbon. Graphene is a form of carbon, and it has a lot of good properties. I was dumbfounded when I was shopping because of the slogan “graphene underpants”.
　　Graphene is so popular that it was widely used in all kinds of fields. But is graphene really the “universal material”, the chosen one?
　　Due to concerns about human health issues arising from exposure to graphene, we studied the effects of graphene nanopores on mammals. Graphene nanopores is a derivative of graphene, you can think of it as graphene’s sister, or brother if you like.
　　As we all know, when studying humans or mammals, mice are doomed. So we have done experiments on mice, and found that graphene nanopores damage the major organs of mice, such as liver, kidney, lung, heart, brain and testis. Although the graphene nanopores was quickly cleared by these organs, it still showed sub-acute toxicity. Graphene nanopores also has certain toxicity in vitro.
　　These findings will have implications for the commercialization of graphene, but life and health should not be taken lightly. The effects on different animals, different doses and different delivery routes must be considered before any new “wonder material” can be put into clinical use.
